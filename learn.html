Car transmissions are systems that control how power from the engine is transferred to the wheels. Below are the types of manual controls;
1. Manual Transmission (gear - Stick Shift)
- Driver changes gears manually using a clutch pedal and gear stick. - More control, better fuel economy (sometimes), but harder to learn.

2. Automatic Transmission
- Changes gears on its own based on speed and load. - Easier to drive, especially in traffic.(because they don't need clutch pedals or gear shifting, making stop-and-go driving less tiring, smooother, and reduces the risk of stalling) - Common in modern cars.

- No fixed gears ‚Äî uses belts and pulleys for a smooth, seamless acceleration. - Fuel-efficient but can feel less responsive,

üõûFWD vs RWD vs AWD

FWD (Front-Wheel Drive)
- sends power to the front wheels and is great for fuel efficiency and snow or rain.
RWD (Rear-Wheel Drive)
- powers the rear wheels, offering better balance and performance, especially in sporty cars.
AWD (All-Wheel Drive)
- delivers power to all four wheels for improved traction, making it ideal for rough terrain or slippery roads.

üîã How Car Batteries Work

  • Your car battery is the heart of your vehicle's electrical system.

  • It provides the power to start the engine and supplies energy to electrical components like lights, radio, and the onboard computer when the engine isn't running.

  • Most cars use a 12-volt lead-acid battery, which stores energy through a chemical reaction involving lead plates and an acid solution.

  • A healthy battery should last around 3 to 5 years, depending on driving habits, climate, and maintenance.

  • Hot weather, short trips,and letting the battery discharge too often can shorten its life.

  • Signs of a weak or failing battery include;
    1. slow engine cranking,
    2. dim lights,
    3. clicking sounds when starting,
    4. and when the battery warning light on your dashboard turns on while you're driving.

  • To maintain your battery, check for corrosion on terminals, keep connections tight, and ensure it‚Äôs securely mounted.

  • If your car won‚Äôt start, you might need a jump-start or a battery replacement.

  • Investing in a multimeter or having your battery tested during regular service can help prevent unexpected breakdowns.
